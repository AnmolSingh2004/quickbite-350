# Quick Bite Website Project

Overview - this is a meal prep service (it is a basic website)

---

# Part 1: Content

## 1. What is the name of your business?
Quick Bite

## 2. Is the business real or fictional?
Fictional

## 3. What products or services does the business offer?
Quick Bite offers healthy meal prep meals, weekly meal plans, and ready-to-eat food options.

## 4. Who is the target audience?
The target audience is college students, busy workers, and families who want convenient healthy meals.

## 5. What problem does this business solve?
It helps people save time while still eating healthier meals.

## 6. What makes this business unique compared to competitors?
Quick Bite offers healthy meals, affordable prices, and a simple ordering experience.

## 7. What pages will your website include?
Home, Meals, FAQ, and Contact.

## 8. Write a short mission statement for the business.
Quick Bite’s mission is to make healthy eating easy, affordable, and convenient.

## 9. What tone should the website content have?
Friendly, simple, and professional.

## 10. What call-to-action do you want visitors to take?
View meals and contact the business.

## 11. What information must be immediately visible on the homepage?
The homepage shows the logo, navigation, a strong heading, meal photos, and a call-to-action button.

## 12. Will the site include images, videos, or other media? Why?
Yes, it includes food images and a logo because meal pictures are important for a food website.

---

# Part 2: Design

## 1. What overall style will the website have?
Clean, modern, and organized.

## 2. What color scheme will you use and why?
A green color theme with white backgrounds is used because green matches healthy food, freshness, and meal prep.

## 3. What fonts will you use for headings and body text?
Arial for both headings and body text.

## 4. How will spacing and layout improve readability?
The design uses section spacing, centered headings, equal meal card spacing, and clear footer sections.

## 5. What visual hierarchy will guide the user’s attention?
Users first see the large homepage heading, then the featured meal images, then the benefits section.

## 6. How will consistency be maintained across pages?
The same header, footer, color theme, card style, and layout style are used on every page.

## 7. What icons will you use?
No extra icons are used in this version.

## 8. What emotions should the design evoke in users?
Trust, freshness, comfort, and interest in the meals.

## 9. How will accessibility be considered?
The design uses readable font sizes, image alt text, clear contrast, and a responsive layout.

## 10. What inspiration sources influenced your design?
The homepage wireframe and the earlier layout version that was already created.

## 11. Describe the layout of the homepage and build the wireframe
The homepage includes a header with logo and navigation, a hero section, featured meals, a benefits section, and a footer with more information.

## 12. Wireframe
The wireframe image is included in the images folder.

# Part 3: Interactivity

## 1. What interactive elements will your site include?
Navigation links, buttons, and a contact form.

## 2. How will users navigate the website?
Users navigate with the menu at the top of each page.

## 3. Will there be form validation or user feedback? Explain.
The contact form uses built-in HTML required fields.

## 4. What functionality will you implement?
The site uses HTML and CSS only.

## 5. How does interactivity improve user experience?
It helps users move around the site and contact the business easily.
